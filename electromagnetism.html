Electromagnetism

Electromagnetism unifies electricity and magnetism into one fundamental force. It governs everything from the behavior of atoms to the generation of electricity and the propagation of light.

Electric Fields and Forces

Electric charges create electric fields. Like charges repel, unlike charges attract. Coulomb's Law: F = k(qq/r) describes the force between charges. Electric field strength E = F/q measures force per unit charge.

Electric Current and Circuits

Current (I) is the flow of charge, measured in Amperes. Voltage (V) is electrical potential difference. Ohm's Law: V = IR relates voltage, current, and resistance. Power P = VI shows energy transfer rate.

Magnetic Fields

Moving charges create magnetic fields. The force on a moving charge in a magnetic field is F = qvB sin(). Magnetic field lines flow from North to South poles. Earth itself is a giant magnet.

Electromagnetic Induction

Faraday's Law: changing magnetic flux induces voltage ( = -d/dt). This principle powers generators and transformers. Lenz's Law states induced currents oppose the change causing them, conserving energy.
